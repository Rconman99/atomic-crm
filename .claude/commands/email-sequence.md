# /email-sequence — Personalized Outbound Email Sequence

Generate a researched, personalized email sequence. Not templates — custom emails based on real context.

## Instructions

The user will provide: target company, contact name/title, and optionally the pain point or angle.

### Step 1: Research
- Search for the company: recent news, funding, job postings, tech stack
- Search for the contact: role, background, content they've shared
- Identify 2-3 specific hooks (personalization points)

### Step 2: Generate the Sequence

Create a 4-email sequence. Each email should be:
- Under 150 words (short emails get higher response rates)
- One clear CTA (call to action) per email
- Personalized with specific details from research
- Written at a conversational, peer-to-peer level (not salesy)

```
═══ EMAIL SEQUENCE ═══
Target: [Contact Name], [Title] at [Company]
Angle: [Primary pain point / hook]

─── EMAIL 1: The Hook (Day 1) ───
Subject: [Short, specific, curiosity-driven]

[Body — 100-150 words max]
[Reference something specific about them]
[Connect it to a pain point]
[Soft CTA: "Would it make sense to explore this?"]

─── EMAIL 2: The Value (Day 3) ───
Subject: Re: [Previous subject] — or new angle

[Body — 80-120 words]
[Share a specific result or insight]
[Social proof from similar company/role]
[CTA: "Happy to share how [similar company] did this"]

─── EMAIL 3: The Pivot (Day 7) ───
Subject: [New angle entirely]

[Body — 80-100 words]
[Different pain point or approach]
[Show you understand their world]
[CTA: "Even a 15-minute call — worth it?"]

─── EMAIL 4: The Breakup (Day 14) ───
Subject: Should I close your file?

[Body — 50-80 words]
[Honest, direct, no guilt-tripping]
[Leave the door open]
[CTA: "If timing's off, just let me know"]
```

### Step 3: Personalization Checklist

Verify each email has:
- [ ] Their name (not just company name)
- [ ] Something specific to THEM (not generic industry stuff)
- [ ] A reason WHY NOW (timeliness)
- [ ] A clear, low-friction CTA
- [ ] No jargon, buzzwords, or "synergy"

### Rules
- NEVER use "I hope this email finds you well" — instant delete trigger
- NEVER use "just checking in" — provides zero value
- NEVER make claims you can't back up with specific data
- Short paragraphs. 1-2 sentences each. White space is your friend.
- Subject lines: 3-7 words. Lowercase. Specific, not clickbait.
- If you don't have enough research to personalize, say so — generic outreach is worse than no outreach.
