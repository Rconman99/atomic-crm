# /meeting-prep — AI-Powered Sales Meeting Preparation

Research a prospect thoroughly before every call. Never walk into a meeting unprepared.

## Instructions

The user will provide a company name and optionally a contact name. Build a comprehensive meeting prep brief.

### Step 1: Company Research
Use web search to find:
- **Company overview:** What they do, industry, size, headquarters, founding year
- **Recent news:** Last 3-6 months of press releases, funding, acquisitions, leadership changes
- **Tech stack signals:** Job postings mentioning specific technologies, BuiltWith data
- **Financial signals:** Revenue estimates, funding rounds, growth indicators
- **Company culture:** Glassdoor highlights, mission statement, values

### Step 2: Contact Research (if name provided)
Search for:
- **LinkedIn summary:** Role, tenure at company, career history
- **Previous companies:** Where they came from (context for their perspective)
- **Content they've shared:** Articles, posts, talks (shows what they care about)
- **Mutual connections:** Anyone in your network who knows them

### Step 3: Pain Point Hypotheses
Based on your research, hypothesize 3 pain points they likely face:
1. **[Industry-specific challenge]** — Why you think this applies to them
2. **[Role-specific challenge]** — Based on their title and responsibilities
3. **[Timing-specific challenge]** — Based on recent news or company stage

### Step 4: Competitive Landscape
- What tools/platforms are they likely using today?
- What alternatives might they be evaluating?
- What's your unique advantage for their specific situation?

### Step 5: Conversation Strategy
Build the brief:

```
═══ MEETING PREP BRIEF ═══

Company: [Name]
Contact: [Name, Title]
Meeting: [Date/Time if provided]

─── COMPANY SNAPSHOT ───
[2-3 sentence overview]
Key metrics: [employees, revenue estimate, funding]
Recent news: [top 3 headlines with dates]

─── CONTACT SNAPSHOT ───
[Role, tenure, background]
Likely priorities: [based on role + company context]

─── PAIN POINT HYPOTHESES ───
1. [Hypothesis + evidence]
2. [Hypothesis + evidence]
3. [Hypothesis + evidence]

─── COMPETITIVE CONTEXT ───
Current tools: [what they likely use]
Our advantage: [specific to their situation]

─── CONVERSATION STARTERS ───
1. "[Personalized opener based on recent news]"
2. "[Question based on pain point hypothesis]"
3. "[Industry insight that demonstrates expertise]"

─── DISCOVERY QUESTIONS ───
1. [Open-ended question about their current process]
2. [Question that reveals pain points]
3. [Question about decision-making process]
4. [Question about timeline and urgency]
5. [Question about budget and authority]

─── NEXT STEPS TO PROPOSE ───
- [Specific next step based on meeting type]
- [Backup next step if primary doesn't land]
```

### Step 6: Save the Brief
Save to a file the user can reference during the call.

### Rules
- Be SPECIFIC, not generic. "They're probably looking to grow" is useless. "They just raised Series B and are hiring 15 engineers, suggesting rapid scaling" is valuable.
- Cite your sources. "According to their recent press release..." builds credibility.
- Keep the brief scannable — you'll be glancing at it during a live call.
- Update AFTER the call with what you learned for next time.
