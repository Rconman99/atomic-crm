# /battle-card — Competitive Battle Card Generator

Build an intelligence brief on a specific competitor. Know them better than they know themselves.

## Instructions

The user will name a competitor. Research and build a comprehensive battle card.

### Step 1: Research the Competitor
Use web search to find:
- Product overview and key features
- Pricing model and tiers
- Recent product launches or updates
- Customer reviews (G2, Capterra, TrustPilot)
- Known customers and case studies
- Company size, funding, growth trajectory
- Leadership team and their background

### Step 2: Build the Battle Card

```
═══ COMPETITIVE BATTLE CARD ═══
Competitor: [Name]
Last Updated: [Date]

─── OVERVIEW ───
What they do: [2-3 sentences]
Founded: [Year] | HQ: [Location] | Size: [Employees]
Funding: [Total raised, last round]
Key customers: [Notable logos]

─── THEIR STRENGTHS (be honest) ───
1. [Genuine strength] — How they'll position this
2. [Another strength]
3. [Another strength]

─── THEIR WEAKNESSES (our opportunities) ───
1. [Real weakness] — Evidence (reviews, missing features)
2. [Another weakness]
3. [Another weakness]

─── PRICING ───
Model: [Per seat / usage / flat rate]
Entry point: $[X]/mo
Enterprise: $[X]/mo
vs Our Pricing: [How we compare]

─── COMMON OBJECTIONS THEY RAISE ABOUT US ───

Objection 1: "[What they say about us]"
→ Reframe: "[How to respond with evidence]"

Objection 2: "[What they say]"
→ Reframe: "[How to respond]"

Objection 3: "[What they say]"
→ Reframe: "[How to respond]"

─── QUESTIONS TO ASK WHEN THEY'RE IN THE DEAL ───
1. "[Question that highlights their weakness]"
2. "[Question about a capability we have that they don't]"
3. "[Question about long-term scalability/cost]"
4. "[Question about integration/ecosystem]"

─── WIN/LOSS PATTERNS ───
We WIN when: [conditions that favor us]
We LOSE when: [conditions that favor them]
Toss-up when: [neutral scenarios]

─── TRAP QUESTIONS (they'll ask about us) ───
Question: "[What they'll ask to make us look bad]"
Answer: "[How to handle it confidently]"

─── LANDMINES TO SET ───
(Requirements to introduce early that favor us)
1. "[Requirement that's easy for us, hard for them]"
2. "[Another requirement]"
3. "[Another requirement]"
```

### Step 3: Save and Date
Save the battle card with today's date. Competitive intel gets stale fast — plan to refresh monthly.

### Rules
- Be HONEST about competitor strengths. If they're genuinely good at something, say so.
  Credibility comes from honesty, not from pretending competitors are bad.
- Cite sources. "According to G2 reviews..." or "Their docs show..."
- Focus on what MATTERS TO THE BUYER, not what matters to us.
- Refresh every 30 days. Competitors ship fast.
